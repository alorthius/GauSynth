GauSynth:
Diffusion-based Reimagination for 3D Object Synthesis with Gaussian Splatting

Anna-Alina Bondarets1,2, Taras Rumezhak1,2
1Ukrainian Catholic University, 2SoftServe
Code

GauSynth performs upon input video (left) to reimagine and reconstruct the 3D asset (right).

Abstract

The reimagination concept is initially defined for 2D image transformation to enhance or alter its appearance; we shift this notion to 3D objects to re-create a similar asset with a modified look. While there are approaches addressing it, they are often associated with expensive finetuning to ensure consistency, the reliance on outdated 2D generative models, and the low output resolution.

We present our ultimate 3D reimagination pipeline GauSynth, composed of the recent 2D generative model SD-XL, 3D Gaussian Splatting for geometry reconstruction, and Super-Resolution upscaling model Swin2SR to address the low-quality issue of the existing 3D generative models. The proposed system generalizes to various inputs, such as renderings of digital 3D assets or turn-around videos of a real-life object.

We also explore the built-in capabilities of the state-of-the-art 2D generative model SD-XL for consistent multi-frame view reimagination. We show that the consistent shots are not the modelâ€™s out-of-distribution data, thus leveraging it with no posterior finetuning by using the introduced model sheet hack in a single inference manner.

Architecture

The proposed pipeline of GauSynth has two major branches:

  1. 2D Reimagination: Incorporates SD-XL model with advanced controlling mechanisms of ControlNet with PyraCanny Edges and IP-Adapter, interpolation model to apply the reimagination to all the input frames, and Super Resolution upscaler to enhance the quality.

  2. 3D Reconstruction: Responses for estimating the underlying 3D geometry via the underlying Structure from Motion and 3D Gaussian Splatting optimization algorithms.
